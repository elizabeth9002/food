pounded yam and egusi

ingredients

  • tuber yam
  • 1 lbs boneless chicken cut in bit-size pieces
  • 1 medium sliced onion
  • 2 pieces maggi cubes
  • 2 fresh chile peppers
  • 2 cups melon seeds
  • 340g ugwu
  • 1/2 cup palm oil
  • 1 cup vegetable oil
  • dreied fish
  • salt
  • crayfish
  • dried shrimp[optional]

preparation procedures

    INSTRUCTIONS

    Method 1 using mortar and pestle,

  1. peel the yam with a sharp knife. remove any spoiled spots or discoloration.
  2. chop into large chunks and immediately pour into water and wash dirt off.
  3. Pour yams in a medium pot under high heat with water- enough to cover the yams. add salt to taste if desied.
  4. Boil until tender for about 20 minutes. Remove from heat and immediately drain yams thoroughly in a colander
  5. Wash your mortar and pestle until clean. Add cooked yam to mortar and pound until smooth and stretchy.
  6. Add little water when necessary during pounding.
  7. Roll into balls and wrap in a plastic wrap or serve immediately in bowl with soup.
    8. STEPS FOR MAKING EGUSI SOUP
  8. Put chicken pieces in a medium pot. Rinse the dried fish and add it to the pot.
  9. Add 1 onion, salt to taste and 2 maggi cubes.
  10. Add 1 1/2 cup water and bring to a boil on high heat for 25 minutes. Reduce heat and simmer for 30 min.
  11. Blend chile peppers and the remaining onion in a blender and puree. Pour onion pepper mixture into a bowl and set aside.
  12. Put shrimp, crayfish and melon seed in a clean blender and pulse until a powder forms. Pour it in a bowl and set aside.
  13. Squeeze the excess liquid from the spinach and set aside.
  14. Continue to cook until the chicken is tender. Stir in blended onion pepper mixture and cook 10 minutes. Reduce heat to medium and add the melon seed mixture.
  15. Cook 20 minutes longer and add the spinach, oils and the 1 remaining maggi cube Cover it and cook for another 5 minutes.
